## Description

### Exploration Objectives

*What young people will have done, questioned or produced by the end of the session. Frame in terms of lived experience, not acquired competencies. Avoid abstract verbs: understand, grasp, raise awareness. Prefer: having handled, having debated, having produced, having noticed that...*

### Hook and Playfulness

*What makes the activity fun or exciting for young people. Not the learning objectives - what hooks them, what surprises them, what makes them want to participate.*

### Relevance for Youth Work

Youth workers, educators and facilitators meet young people at a moment when identities are forming and futures are being imagined. Introducing AI in these spaces is not a neutral act: it is an opportunity to help young people feel capable of understanding complex systems and taking part in debates that concern them. AI is already in their daily lives (recommendations, filters, automated decisions), yet often remains invisible or misunderstood. This activity opens space to study what these tools do, who designed them, for whom, and with what values.
